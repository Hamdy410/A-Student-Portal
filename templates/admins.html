Instructions:

Hello and Welcome. This is the admins page, where you can monitor the entire portal's activity from registering new members to sending alerts to other individuals. Also, you can manage the Media (Posts and Replies) through out the site.

In the next few lines, We will introduce you to the main tasks and rules of becoming an admin in our systems.

1) Registering Students:

Each semester, We receive paper copies of our new students through the Admission Office, so the first task you will be assigned will be inserting (registering) students' required information and update their status as per order from the Admission Office. However, registering the faculty staff is not part of your duty as they are not regulated via faculty but by the head university.

As an admin, A part of your duty is to keep the students' information as secure as possible. Following themes of Confidentiality, Integrity, and Accountability is crucial to guarantee the safety of our students.

2) Media Management:

As for the presence of our social service with the availability of alerts, posts, and replies, admins need to regulate the best friendly-formal atmosphere for all users. That means that your task is to monitor reports on media to ensure they follow our privacy and policies.

As an admin, You have the complete administration of the interaction of users with the system and each other.

3) Alerts:

Your mission is to secure bot the systems and the web content. That means you must periodically monitor this section and the number of active and passive reports.

In Alerts, you have three tasks: Send Alerts (to students, faculty staff, or other admins), Receive alerts, and monitor users' reports (regarding issues, posts, and replies among the media).

4) Posts:

In this section, you can see the posts and post ones.

[Note: Since admins are users of high authority, their posts are considered a priority. If an announcement is crucial to post, We advise posting via a single admin (not more than one.)]


Rules:

1) Least Privilege Principle: Admins should only have access they need to perform their duties. This reduces the risk of accidental changes or security breaches.

2) Change Management: Any changes to the system should be documented and approved before being committed. This helps track what was done and why for future administrators.

3) Security Practices: Admins should follow secure practices, like using strong, unique passwords, and not sharing their credentials.

4) Regular Updates: Keep the system updated to be protected against possible vulnerabilities. It's crucial to report any possible breaches, glitches or malfunctions as soon as possible.

5) Training: All admins are ensured to be properly trained and understand their responsibilities.

6) Incident Response: Have a plan for responding to security incidents, and make sure all admins are familiar with it.

7) Backup and Recovery: Regularly backup important data and have a plan for recovering our data loss.

8) Compliance: Ensure all actions are in compliance with relevant laws and regulations.
