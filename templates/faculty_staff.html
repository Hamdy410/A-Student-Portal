Instructions:

Hello and welcome. This is the Faculty Staff page, where you can communicate with your Students & Admins. From here, you can regulate your courses, see the Media (Posts and Replies) and receive alerts from the users of higher authorities – in your case: Admins – through the site

In the next few lines, We will introduce you to the main tasks and rules of becoming a faculty staff in our systems.

1) Courses:

From here, you can have the opportunity to regulate your current assigned courses, and the course enrollments regarding your course. You are shown the number of students who have requested to enroll in your course, but since the final list of students is given by the University administration, you cannot see the names of students who have enrolled in your specific courses.

2) Media:

As for the presence of our social service where you can share your posts and see communication and replies of other users regarding your posts. Currently, the system supports written text, and of course your favorite emojis are also supported.

There you will find an environment of support, so feel free to ask all your questions here and we are here to answer you.

[Note: Any act of misconduct might resolve in completely deleting your post and/or your suspension from the system either temporarily or permanently. In that case, please head to the Students' Affairs Office.

3) Alerts:

Your mission is to secure bot the systems and the web content. That means you must periodically monitor this section and the number of active alerts from admins.

In Alerts, you have the ability to send alerts only to students.


Rules:

1) Least Privilege Principle: Faculty Staff should only have access they need to perform their duties. This reduces the risk of accidental changes or security breaches.

2) Security Practices: Faculty Staff should follow secure practices, like using strong, unique passwords, and not sharing their credentials.

3) Regular Updates: Keep the system updated to be protected against possible vulnerabilities. It's crucial to report any possible breaches, glitches or malfunctions as soon as possible.

4) Training: All faculty staff are ensured to be properly trained and understand their responsibilities.

5) Incident Response: Have a plan for responding to security incidents, and make sure all faculty staff are familiar with it.

6) Compliance: Ensure all actions are in compliance with relevant laws and regulations.
