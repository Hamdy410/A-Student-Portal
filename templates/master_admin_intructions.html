Instructions:

Hello and Welcome. This is the Master Admin Control Unit, where you can have the superior control over the high leveled users on the portal.

In the next few lines, You will be introduced to the main control systems of this part of the system.

1) New Users:

As per higher-leveled users should be regulated more carefully due to their high influence on the environment of the system. Users like Faculty Staff Members and fellow Admins must be regulated simultaneously and more carefully. They are part of the team and so you're responsible for their registrations.

As for being the Head Developer of the system it is important to achieve integrity, Confidentiality and Accountability in dealing with new members and regulate their insertion beside guiding their first hands-on interaction with the system

2) Courses:

Academic Courses are extremely important and updated each year. For that reason, this section would be used on the range of the entire year to make regulate the students' enrollment in the variety of courses.

3) Suspensions:

Sometimes, it would be required to suspend certain users, either because they were admins within your team who are no longer functioning in their position. Also, Faculty Staff Members and Students could be suspended from accessing the system for a variety of reason.

Your responsibility is to regulate the users' suspensions and their regulations.

Note: You shall not use the functionalities of this system without a written permission from the University board. In case of any inconvenience, it would be regulated as fast as possible.

4) Alert to all:

In this section, you can find a form used to send an alert to all the users at the same time.

Rules:

1) Least Privilege Principle: Admins should only have access they need to perform their duties. This reduces the risk of accidental changes or security breaches.

2) Change Management: Any changes to the system should be documented and approved before being committed. This helps track what was done and why for future administrators.

3) Security Practices: Admins should follow secure practices, like using strong, unique passwords, and not sharing their credentials.

4) Regular Updates: Keep the system updated to be protected against possible vulnerabilities. It's crucial to report any possible breaches, glitches or malfunctions as soon as possible.

5) Training: All admins are ensured to be properly trained and understand their responsibilities.

6) Incident Response: Have a plan for responding to security incidents, and make sure all admins are familiar with it.

7) Backup and Recovery: Regularly backup important data and have a plan for recovering our data loss.

8) Compliance: Ensure all actions are in compliance with relevant laws and regulations.
