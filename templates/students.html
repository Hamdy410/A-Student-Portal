Instructions:

Hello and Welcome. This is the students page, where you can communicate with our Faculty Staff & Admins. From here you can regulate your enrolled courses, see the Media (Posts and Replies) and receive alerts from other users through the site.

In the next few lines, We will introduce you to the main tasks and rules of becoming a student in our systems.

1) Media:

As for the presence of our social service where you can share your posts and see communication and replies of other users regarding your posts. Currently, the system supports written text, and of course your favorite emojis are also supported.

There you will find an environment of support, so feel free to ask all your questions here and we are here to answer you.

[Note: Any act of misconduct might resolve in completely deleting your post and/or your suspension from the system either temporarily or permanently. In that case, please head to the Students' Affairs Office.

2) Courses:

From here you will have the opportunity to regulate your course enrollments to see the course titles and faculty lecturer. You will receive courses suitable for your year and semester.

Please note that your enrollment is just a request, so you are free to enrol with multiple lecturers for the same course or material. However, only one would be accepted according to: the number of students per course, your grades whether during the last year (either school or college) and/or the grades in your last semester (in case of a course series). It is check the results of your enrollments at the Students Affairs Office one week before the start of your first course.

3) Alerts:

From here, you can receive alerts from users of higher authority. From your the Faculty Staff or Admins.


Rules:

1) Least Privilege Principle: Students should only have access they need to perform their duties. This reduces the risk of accidental changes or security breaches.

2) Security Practices: Students should follow secure practices, like using strong, unique passwords, and not sharing their credentials.

3) Regular Updates: Keep the system updated to be protected against possible vulnerabilities. It's crucial to report any possible breaches, glitches or malfunctions as soon as possible.

4) Compliance: Ensure all actions are in compliance with relevant laws and regulations.
